The Smoke-Controlled Public Zones

How the Idea Works

Concept Overview:

Public smoking zones will be strategically located in high foot traffic areas, such as parks, public squares, or near transport hubs, where smokers can access them without disrupting non-smokers. Access Mechanism: Individuals wishing to use these zones will pay a nominal fee (₹10 for 5 minutes) via a digital payment system or a token machine at the entrance. This fee not only manages the flow of users but also contributes to the maintenance of the zones. Usage Regulation: Each zone will have a set limit on the number of users allowed at any given time, ensuring that air quality and comfort are maintained. Users will be notified when their time is about to expire, encouraging them to be mindful of their smoking habits.


The Smoke-Controlled Public Zones aim to designate specific areas for smoking in public spaces, ensuring that smoking occurs in controlled environments equipped with smoke purification technologies.

⁠Benefits of the Initiative

Public Health Improvements:

Reduced Exposure: By concentrating smoking in specific areas equipped with air purification technology, the zones significantly reduce secondhand smoke exposure for non-smokers in public spaces. Healthier Environment: Cleaner air in surrounding areas leads to improved public health outcomes, particularly for vulnerable populations such as children, pregnant women, and individuals with respiratory conditions. Behavioral Changes:


Encouraging Responsibility: The nominal access fee promotes responsible smoking behavior, encouraging users to be more mindful of their habits and consider the impact on others.

Community and Institutional Benefits:
Awareness Campaigns: The zones can also serve as educational platforms where information about the risks of smoking and benefits of cessation can be shared. Environmental Protection: Sustainable Practices: The initiative aligns with broader environmental goals by minimizing pollution in public areas, contributing to cleaner urban environments.

Technical Approach

Smoke Purification Technology:

Filtration Systems: Each smoking zone will be equipped with state-of-the-art HEPA (High-Efficiency Particulate Air) filters and activated carbon filters. These systems work by: HEPA Filters: Capturing fine particulate matter, including smoke particles, allergens, and other pollutants. Activated Carbon Filters: Adsorbing gases and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) present in tobacco smoke, effectively neutralizing harmful substances. Chemical Scrubbers: In addition to traditional filtration, advanced chemical scrubbers may be used to treat smoke with chemical reactions that break down toxic components, ensuring cleaner air is released back into the environment.


Real-Time Monitoring Systems: Air Quality Sensors: Sensors will continuously monitor air quality, measuring levels of particulate matter, CO2, and other pollutants. This data will inform users and authorities about the effectiveness of the purification systems. Usage Tracking: Cameras and sensors will monitor user count and behavior within the zones. This data will be valuable for assessing peak usage times, overall effectiveness, and areas for improvement. Feedback Mechanisms: Users will have the ability to provide feedback through a mobile app, which will contribute to ongoing improvements in the operation of the zones.
